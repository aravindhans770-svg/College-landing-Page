Our story

Built by people who'd rather make than lecture.

TechNova started as a 40-person night program for working engineers in 2014. Twelve years later, it's a full college — same studio instinct, much bigger floor.

Mission

Teach the work, not just the theory behind it.

Most engineering education optimizes for exams. We optimize for the first six months of a real job — ambiguous specs, broken builds, and a team waiting on your part of the system. Every course at TechNova is built backward from a working artifact: a deployed app, a tested circuit, a shipped prototype.

That means smaller lecture halls, longer studio hours, and faculty who are still active in the field they teach.

How we got here.

2014

A night program for 40 engineers

Founded in a rented floor above a Seoul machine shop, teaching working engineers after hours.

2017

First accredited degree program

Launched the B.S. in Computing and moved into our current Innovation Drive campus.

2020

Research labs open

Founded our first three research labs in robotics, applied ML, and climate systems.

2026

4,200 students, 60+ industry partners

Four schools, twelve labs, and a placement rate we publish every year — good or bad.

What we hold to

Three commitments that don't move.

Published outcomes

Placement rates, salary bands, and graduation rates are public on our site every year, not just in good years.

Faculty who still build

Every instructor maintains active industry or research work — no full-time lecturers teaching only from slides.

Cohorts capped at 24

No studio course runs larger than 24 students. If demand grows, we open a section — we don't enlarge the room.
